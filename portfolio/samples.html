Creative Writing Focus Piece on an Alternate Perspective of an Excerpt

This piece of creative writing was also written for my English 359 class, but instead of writing critically or analytically, I instead chose to write creatively and take on the perspective of John Watson instead of Sherlock Holmes for a passage in the novel by SIr Arthur Conan Doyle, “The Sign of Four”. This piece was the for the culmination of my class, where we were allowed to choose to write analytically or creatively. Since my first paper for the class was analytical, I chose to exercise my creative writing skills and apply my readings to writing in an alternate perspective.

Proposal for A More Accessibility Focused Museum Guide

As part of a group project in my Junior Year Writing class, my group mates and I designed and presented an updated and rethought museum guide for the UMass Museum of Contemporary Art. We kept in mind accessibility standards for patrons looking to visit the museum, guests who are navigating the museum, and for people with disabilities. This project highlights the skills I learned in this class, titled “Writing for Nonprofits”, where we explored many forms of writing and the ways in which they interact with businesses.
